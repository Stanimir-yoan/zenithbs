Understanding Your Consumer Unit

You might call it a fuse box, but today, the heart of your home’s electrical system is known as the consumer unit. This critical component powers all electrical items in your house, including sockets, lights, immersion heaters, electric ovens, hobs, smoke alarms, electric vehicle chargers, and outdoor power. More importantly, the consumer unit provides a vital safety function, protecting you from electric shocks, burns, and fires in the event of a fault.

Essential Safety

The consumer unit safeguards your home by preventing electric shocks, burns, and fires during electrical faults.

Powers Your Home

It supports all electrical items, from lights and sockets to ovens and electric vehicle chargers.

Regular Checks

Electrical installations should be inspected every 10 years, or every 5 years for rented properties.

Is It Time for a New Consumer Unit?

Moving into a new home or noticing an outdated rewireable “fuse box”? It’s the perfect time to assess whether your consumer unit is still keeping you safe. Electrical installations should be checked every 10 years (or 5 years for rented properties) to ensure they function properly and meet modern safety standards.
